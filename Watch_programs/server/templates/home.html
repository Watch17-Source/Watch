WATCH

Wireless Alarm and Threat-Check Hub for Classrooms is a LAN-based security system designed to help school guards monitor classrooms at night and respond faster to intrusions.

Why this exists

  • Repeated school ransacking incidents in previous years
  • Guards need faster room-level visibility without walking room-to-room
  • No internet required — local-only network

How it works

  1. Each classroom has an ESP32‑S3 + RCWL‑0516 device connected to the local Wi‑Fi/LAN.
  2. At curfew (trigger time), devices auto-calibrate (empty-room baseline) and arm.
  3. If motion is detected in a room during curfew, the device alarms and reports to this hub.
  4. Guards acknowledge the alert in Watch; the device stops alarming and pauses for inspection.
  5. All alarm sessions are recorded in Cases.

Note: RCWL‑0516 is a Doppler motion sensor; WATCH is engineered to reduce false alarms and stabilize detection, but motion sensors cannot perfectly distinguish humans from all other movement.
